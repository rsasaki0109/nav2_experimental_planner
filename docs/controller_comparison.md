# Local controller comparison (VFH+ / ND / learned, transformer, recurrent & hybrid Mode A)

Auto-generated by `nav2_planner_benchmarks` (`ros2 run nav2_planner_benchmarks controller_benchmark`). Each controller is rolled out closed-loop against a live `nav2_costmap_2d::Costmap2DROS` (6x6 m, 0.05 m) with a unicycle motion model (dt=0.1 s, up to 300 steps), following a straight global plan while reacting to obstacles. No Gazebo/GPU.

All are `nav2_core::Controller` plugins absent from upstream Nav2 (which ships DWB / MPPI / Regulated Pure Pursuit). Two are reactive: **VFH+** picks a free histogram valley by cost; **ND** selects a navigable gap and adds a safety deflection that centres the robot between close obstacles. The other two are generative: **Diffusion (Mode A, learned)** loads the curated costmap-conditioned flow trajectory model from `model_zoo` via `OnnxTrajectoryModel` (real ONNX inference) — it *proposes* K trajectories that lean away from a one-sided obstacle (verified end-to-end in the `nav2_diffusion_onnx` test), a deterministic kinematic + footprint layer *disposes* of unsafe ones, and the controller drives the best. As a small synthetic-trained research model it **reaches the goal in *open*** but on the obstacle scenarios (outside its training distribution) the safety layer stops it safely short of the block. **Diffusion (Mode A, transformer)** is the same controller driven by the transformer model_zoo sibling (DETR-style set-prediction, identical ONNX contract) — a same-contract drop-in proposer. **Diffusion (Mode A, recurrent)** is another such drop-in: the GRU autoregressive-rollout model_zoo sibling that emits the trajectory one point at a time (same contract, sequential inductive bias). **Diffusion (Mode A, hybrid)** adds a classical `fallback_controller_plugin` (here VFH+): when no learned candidate is safe it delegates instead of stopping, so it **reaches the goal in every scenario** — learned drives the easy cases, the classical controller threads the obstacles. This is the Mode A analogue of the hybrid Mode B planner. See its [model card](../model_zoo/diffusion_local/model_card.md) and [../docs/generative_limits.md](../docs/generative_limits.md).

_Min clearance is the smallest robot-to-obstacle distance over the run (higher = safer). Mean |dw| is the average step-to-step change in angular velocity (lower = smoother). Mean |y-centre| (corridor only) is how far from the corridor centreline the robot stayed (lower = better centred). The two reactive controllers (VFH+, ND) clear every scenario, trading berth (VFH+ tends to give wider clearance) against smoothness (ND tends to steer more smoothly). The **learned** Mode A controller reaches the goal in *open* but, as a small research model, safe-stops short on the obstacle scenarios (timeout, no collision). The **transformer** and **recurrent** Mode A models (same ONNX contract, different proposer architectures — set-prediction and GRU rollout) behave equivalently — reach *open*, safe-stop on the obstacle scenarios — confirming the ceiling is the synthetic data / capacity, not the model family. The **hybrid** adds a classical fallback (VFH+) that takes over when no learned candidate is safe, so it **reaches the goal in every scenario** — on the corridor its row matches VFH+ exactly, i.e. the fallback drove. The learned proposals are verified end-to-end in the `nav2_diffusion_onnx` test; in the loop the safety layer keeps both collision-free throughout. "timeout" means the goal was not reached within the step budget; "collision" means the robot entered a lethal cell._
